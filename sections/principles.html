Guiding Principles

The architecture of FORAP is guided by core PjBL pedagogical principles and design goals that translate the identified needs of instructors and students into actionable directions for the framework. Together, these principles guide how projects are packaged to support reuse and adaptation, reduce adoption effort, support student progress, and strengthen assessment.

PjBL Principles

Authentic Learning
Projects should connect learning to realistic contexts, tasks, and practices so that students engage with meaningful and relevant project work.
PJP1
Active Learning
Projects should position students as active participants in learning through sustained hands-on engagement with project tasks, decisions, and problem-solving.
PJP2
Self-Directed Learning
Projects should create opportunities for students to learn, plan, research, make decisions, and work through ambiguity with appropriate support.
PJP3
Collaborative Learning
Projects should create opportunities for teamwork, coordination, communication, and shared responsibility.
PJP4
Critical Thinking
Projects should require meaningful cognitive engagement by asking students to analyze, evaluate, and make informed design and implementation decisions.
PJP5

Design Goals

Aligned Learning Objectives
FORAP should center project design on aligned learning objectives so project tasks, support materials, and assessment remain anchored to explicit and comparable learning expectations.
GOAL1
Reusability and Adaptability
FORAP should package projects in a modular and flexible form that supports reuse across offerings and adaptation to different courses, contexts, constraints, and instructional goals.
GOAL2
Support for Teaching and Learning
FORAP should include coordinated support materials that help instructors deploy projects and help students make progress through common technical and project-task challenges.
GOAL3
Appropriateness of Challenges
Projects should be scoped and structured so that challenges are meaningful and appropriately matched to student preparation, support, and course context.
GOAL4
Formative and Summative Assessment
FORAP should support both continuous feedback during project work and evaluation of what students have learned at the end of the project.
